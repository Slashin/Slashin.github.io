About

Ever since I was young, I've always loved to take things apart and put them back together. Learning about the inner intracacies of things, whether it be a watch or a computer, simply fascinated me. This is why I chose to study Mechatronics Engineering at the University of Waterloo where I hope to recieve a strong theoretical education and an even stronger practical skillset which I can use for the rest of my career as an engineer.

Prolonged curiosity has driven me to learn many new things over the past couple of years. From building a simple website in grade 9, to creating a full fledged, interactive website within a course offered by Coursera last year; I have come a long way in the field of web development. Currently I am quite confident in my HTML and CSS skills and am in the process of improving my knowledge for JavaScript.

In terms of core programming languages, I have over 2 years of experience with C++ and around 6 months of experience Java. I also spent some time learning SQL, because I feel it is very important to know how to access and manipulate the copious amount of data stored within databases in our world today.

My largest and most successful project till date has been the "Piezoelectric Vibration-Sensing Shoe". I was able to learn and apply many essential technical and non-technical skills while creating this project. Specifically, I vastly improved my Arduino knowledge and also learned how to do proper electrical work such as soldering. I also further developed my public speaking and presentation skills during my trip to Montreal for the Canada-Wide Science Fair in 2016, where I had to present my project to both highly qualified judges and public visitors. I'm very glad to have also recieved a gold medal in my regional science fair and a silver medal at the national science fair for this project as well.
